\section*{Abstract}

This report describes a multi-fidelity Bayesian optimisation (MF-BO) framework applied to Ahmed body aerodynamics, combining a 30-case Reynolds-Averaged Navier--Stokes (RANS) design-of-experiments with a 56-case Detached Eddy Simulation (DES) campaign to build a co-Kriging surrogate over a four-dimensional parameter space: rear slant angle ($15\text{--}40^\circ$), underbody diffuser angle ($0\text{--}20^\circ$), ride height ($30\text{--}80$~mm), and nose fillet radius ($50\text{--}150$~mm).

RANS simulations (simpleFoam, $k$-$\omega$~SST, $\mathrm{Re}=2.78\times10^6$) provide low-fidelity coverage of the design space at low computational cost. DES cases (pimpleFoam, $k$-$\omega$~SST-DES with IDDES, $t_\mathrm{end}=0.45$~s) provide high-fidelity correction data; the mean drag coefficient correction is $\delta C_d = +0.017\pm0.023$ with range $[-0.047, +0.080]$, confirming that RANS systematically under-predicts drag but with substantial local variability. The co-Kriging surrogate (Kennedy \& O'Hagan additive model) fuses both fidelity levels; Bayesian optimisation using the Expected Improvement (EI) acquisition function directs 44 automated DES cases toward aerodynamic efficiency improvement, followed by a 12-point local Latin Hypercube sweep in the identified optimum region.

The multi-fidelity optimum is identified at slant $= 38.9^\circ$, diffuser $= 15.9^\circ$, ride height $= 48.9$~mm, nose radius $= 99.6$~mm, yielding $C_d = 0.290\pm0.023$ and $C_l = -0.547\pm0.175$ (downforce). EI plateaued at $\approx 0.024$ across the full campaign, indicating a broad, flat optimum region rather than a sharp minimum; the design space admits a continuum of efficient configurations near slant~$38$--$40^\circ$.

A post-hoc statistical analysis of DES time-averaging reveals only $\approx13$ statistically independent samples per run after autocorrelation correction (integral time scale $\tau_{C_d}\approx12$~ms; averaging window $T=320$~ms), giving $C_d$ 95\% confidence intervals of $\pm0.007$ per case. This sets a resolution floor on surrogate training data and is the primary recommendation for future work.
